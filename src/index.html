    • Research Projects

  • Visualization of Sound | Music Painter
      • My Team and I developed a downloadable software in Python, using PySide/PyQt, sound-device, and PyAudio interfaces, that reads the data of a wav file and converts it to a list of dominate frequencies and spectral data. This data is then used to create a 2-D graphical image using simple geometric shapes, hence giving a single image for the entire clip, as opposed to the usual visualizers built into media players that animate just small segments of the song. The project aimed to research and formulate a product that could take any audio file in wav format, including locally captured live recording, and make a more tangible representation in the form of a graphical representation. This project was inspired by a vision of music as an artform, and so, we set out to transform audible art into visual art with immense user customization, yet with constant use of maximum frequency values and spectral data.
